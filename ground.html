Mint makes for an unexpectedly delightful ground cover. It also serves as a mild deterrent for undesirable pests. It has a tendency to take over, so you'll want to keep it contained. Well-controlled mint is a wonderful addition to gardens in this area.

While Angelina Stonecrop doesn't take well to being stepped on, as long as it has sun and some water it will spread anywhere you want it. It also produces tiny flowers that look like they belong in a fairy garden.

Liriope takes a little time to get established, but once it does, it tolerates full sun well. Liriope is another groundcover you will want to keep contained, because it will overtake flowers.

When opting for mulch in a garden, cedar mulch is a little more expensive than other types of mulch, but it has so many benefits. It lasts longer than other mulches, helps retain water, and serves as a natural repellant for unwanted insects.

Lava rock adds year-round color to your garden and will last longer than wood mulches. Its porous nature helps with moisture distribution while at the same time protecting moisture that is in the garden from evaporating.
